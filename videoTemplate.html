  • These videos are 1-2 minutes!
    Strive for concise clarity.

  • Interact with your audience!
    Appeal to what is already intuitive with examples
    or questions framed within an everyday context.

  • Prioritize the cleanliness of this structure...
    ...but don't let it cramp your style!

  • Consider the visuals that will complement your words.
    Some things can be "said" using imagery. Use this to streamline your script!

  • Test your outline out on a friend!
    Practice talking about your topic even before you start outlining :)

1. Present a motivating question

  • Introduce the misconception / motivating statement
  • Challenge it: is this statement true?
    Ex.
  • A smoggier sky is more polluted than a clearer one.
  • It this 100% true?

2. Explain the fundamental science

  • Describe the scientific concepts
  • Limit this description to what is necessary to understand #3
    Ex.
  • Visual clarity depends on the light path between the object and your eye
  • Particles scatter light, obscuring clarity
  • Light scatter is not just dependent on particle count, but other entities like composition and size

3. Reconnect fundamentals with motivation

  • Return to the motivating question
  • Directly apply fundamentals to tie it all together!
    Ex.
  • The sky appears smoggier when there is more light scatter
  • Humidity can change particle composition and/or size, potentially changing how particles scatter light
  • Therefore, the same number of particles can produce different visibilities -- a smoggier sky doesn't necessary mean more pollutants!
